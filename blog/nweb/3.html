Laptops are packed with functionality that goes beyond typing and browsing the web. One area that often goes unnoticed, but is incredibly important, is the set of special keys built right into the keyboard. These are function keys, shortcuts, and toggles that control hardware-level features of the laptop, all designed to improve your user experience. In this post, we’ll explore some of the more common ones you’ll find, as well as a few that might surprise you.

Let’s start with one of the most frequently misunderstood keys: the Airplane Mode button. If you’ve ever had your internet inexplicably stop working, it might not be your Wi-Fi, it could be that this key was accidentally pressed. Activating Airplane Mode disables all wireless communications, including Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and any built-in cellular connections your laptop may have. It’s a useful feature when flying or conserving power, but it can also be a source of confusion during troubleshooting. If your network suddenly vanishes, it’s a good idea to check whether Airplane Mode is turned on before diving into more complex diagnostics.

Speaking of wireless connectivity, many laptops come with additional keys or shortcuts that allow you to toggle specific types of wireless features. For example, there might be dedicated buttons for Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or cellular data (if your laptop supports a SIM card or LTE modem). These let you selectively disable parts of your wireless stack. Want to use Bluetooth headphones while staying plugged into Ethernet for faster internet? You can disable Wi-Fi and cellular independently to save battery and reduce background scanning.

Next up: display management. If you want to connect your laptop to a second monitor, whether for productivity, gaming, or watching videos, you’ll first need to connect the external display via an HDMI, VGA, or DisplayPort cable. Once connected, some laptops automatically detect and switch the display configuration. If not, you can use a function key (usually accessed via the "Fn" key in combination with one of the F1–F12 keys) to cycle through different display modes:

  • Laptop screen only
  • Duplicate (mirror the same content on both screens)
  • Extend (use the second screen as additional space)
  • Second screen only

Each of these modes serves a specific use case, and being able to quickly switch between them can make working across multiple displays seamless.

Everyday Convenience Keys

Volume control buttons are essential when watching videos or attending virtual meetings. These typically include volume up, volume down, and mute buttons.

Media playback controls are often integrated into the function row as well. With these, you can play, pause, skip forward, or rewind music and videos without needing to click through menus.

One lesser-known but extremely helpful feature is the keyboard backlight toggle. Not every laptop has this feature, but those that do make typing in the dark much easier. If you find yourself watching a video at night or working in a dim room, you can use this key to turn on the keyboard light. Some backlights shine from beneath the keys (called backlighting), while others might have top-down illumination from around the display. Either way, it’s a welcome addition when visibility is limited or your posture prevents proper hand placement.

Another function key worth mentioning is the screen orientation key. This one is more situational but comes in handy when rotating your display or using external monitors in portrait mode. It allows you to quickly switch between landscape, portrait, and the flipped versions of both. For users who frequently adjust their monitor setups or use their laptops in tablet mode (common with 2-in-1 convertible devices), this key is a major convenience.
